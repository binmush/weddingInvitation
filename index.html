The Sacred Poruwa

🪷

An ancient tradition of love

The Poruwa ceremony is the sacred heart of a traditional Sinhalese wedding. On an elaborately decorated wooden platform, bride and groom exchange vows in a ritual that has blessed Sri Lankan unions for centuries — guided by auspicious timing, ancient chants, and the blessings of family.

01

Stepping onto the Poruwa

The couple ascends the sacred platform together, right foot first

02

Betel Leaf Offering

Offering betel leaves to each other symbolizing respect and love

03

Tying the Fingers

Little fingers bound together with a sacred gold thread

04

Water Blessing

Holy water poured over joined hands sealing the eternal bond

05

Jayamangala Gatha

Traditional Buddhist blessings chanted for the couple's prosperity

06

Breaking the Coconut

A coconut is broken to dispel misfortune and invoke blessings
